        Under the Hood

        How it Works

        1

        Collect Metadata

        Capture file name, relative path, size, extension, and modified time from the selected folder.

        2

        Store in AVL Trees

        Each snapshot is inserted into an AVL Tree keyed by file path for O(log n) search, insert, and diff operations.

        3

        Compare & Detect

        Traverse both trees to find added, deleted, and modified nodes, then score behaviour changes.

        4

        Risk Scoring

        Size swings, suspicious extensions, and timestamp anomalies increase the risk score to highlight high-risk files first.

        Runs entirely in your browser. No data leaves your machine.
